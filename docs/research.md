# How QuantAI Helps With Market Research

Markets rarely give you one clean question. A new idea can involve price action, filings, earnings, sector pressure, crypto narratives, macro changes, technical signals, and your own earlier assumptions.

QuantAI helps you keep that research thread together.

Last updated: 2026-07-04

## When QuantAI Is Useful

Use QuantAI when you are trying to understand a stock, crypto asset, market theme, portfolio question, or watchlist change and you want to keep asking follow-up questions without starting over.

Good questions include:

- What changed in this company, asset, or market theme?
- Which technical, fundamental, and quantitative signals matter right now?
- What evidence supports or weakens my current thesis?
- Which assumptions should I check before making a decision?
- What should I watch after earnings, filings, price action, or new market news?

## What You Can Research

### Stocks

Review company context, earnings, filings, valuation questions, market signals, watchlist notes, and thesis changes.

### Crypto Assets

Review crypto assets, bitcoin market questions, technical context, narratives, liquidity changes, and risk factors.

### Signals and Strategies

Compare technical indicators, factor ideas, backtesting questions, and quantitative signals as research inputs.

### Investment Theses

Write down what you believe, what evidence supports it, what could break it, and what you should watch next.

## What QuantAI Does Not Do

QuantAI is not a broker. It does not place trades, route orders, manage portfolios, guarantee returns, or provide personalized financial advice.

That boundary matters. The product is designed to help you think through research, not to replace your judgment.
